Depression affects millions of people worldwide, and for many, conventional treatment like antidepressants and therapy fail to provide adequate relief. Ketamine, traditionally used as an anesthetic, has gained prominence in recent years for its ability to deliver rapid and sustained relief for people with treatment-resistant depression. If you are considering ketamine therapy or wondering how long its effects last, this guide covers everything you need to know about treatment duration, its effects, and what to expect.

## What is Ketamine?

Before discussing the specific treatment of **Ketamine depression duration**, it's essential to understand what ketamine is and its role in a cure for mental illness. Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic that has been used in medical settings since the 1970s. It essentially works by inhibiting several receptors in the brain, most notably the NMDA [receptors in the brain](https://www.isha.health/post/how-does-ketamine-work), increasing glutamate production. This neurotransmitter helps promote synaptic connections between neurons. This process, known as synaptogenesis, is believed to play a role in ketamine’s rapid antidepressant effects. It is not without criticism, but it has shown some auspicious results in [treating mental health conditions](https://www.isha.health/locations/online-at-home-ketamine-therapy-in-arizona), especially for people who have severe or treatment-resistant depression.

Unlike traditional antidepressants, which can take weeks to work, ketamine acts very quickly, providing relief within days or even hours. Its rapid action has made it a valuable option for those with severe depression or suicidal ideation who need immediate relief.

‍

## **How Does Ketamine Work For Depression?**

The exact mechanism by which ketamine relieves depression is still being studied. However, research suggests that by blocking NMDA receptors and increasing glutamate levels, ketamine helps restore neural pathways associated with mood regulation. This leads to rapid improvements in mood, mental clarity, and energy levels.

One of ketamine’s unique benefits is its ability to work quickly, unlike selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), which may take weeks to show noticeable effects. For individuals with severe depression, this rapid relief can be life-changing.

## **Ketamine Treatment Session Duration**

Ketamine can be administered in various forms, including intravenous (IV), intramuscular (IM), oral, subcutaneous, and nasal spray (such as esketamine). At Isha Health, we use [oral ketamine](http://www.isha.health/post/6-research-papers-to-read-about-oral-ketamine-for-depression) for treatment.

-   **IV Ketamine:** Sessions typically last 40 minutes to an hour.
-   **Intramuscular (IM) & Subcutaneous:** Similar duration to IV administration.
-   **Oral Ketamine:** Effects can take longer to set in but typically last a few hours.
-   **Nasal Spray (Esketamine):** Effects begin within 30-45 minutes and last a few hours.

## **How Long Do the Effects of Ketamine Last?**

The duration of ketamine’s antidepressant effects varies from person to person and depends on factors such as depression severity, frequency of treatments, and individual neurobiology. Most people experience benefits lasting several days to weeks after a single session.

‍

#### **Short-Term Effects**

-   **Immediate Effects:** Ketamine’s impact can be felt within 30 minutes to an hour after administration. Many people describe it as an emotional “reset,” with depressive thoughts temporarily fading away.
-   **Sustained Relief (Days to Weeks):** After one session, most individuals experience significant improvement in mood for several days to weeks.

‍

#### **Long-Term Effects**

-   Research shows that repeated ketamine treatments over several weeks can lead to prolonged relief by inducing neuroplasticity, which helps create lasting changes in brain function.
-   Some individuals experience relief for months after completing an entire course of treatments.

‍

### **Ketamine Treatment Frequency**

Ketamine is typically administered in a structured treatment plan to maximize benefits.

-   **Initial Treatment Phase:**  
    -   Typically involves 6-12 sessions over 2-3 weeks.
    -   Treatments are usually scheduled 2-3 times per week to optimize response.
-   **Maintenance Phase:**  
    -   After the initial phase, some patients may require maintenance sessions every few weeks or months.
    -   The frequency of maintenance treatments varies based on individual response.

## **Ketamine For Treatment-Resistant Depression(TRD)**

Ketamine is particularly effective for treatment-resistant depression (TRD), which does not respond well to conventional antidepressants or therapy. Many people with TRD face challenges in achieving satisfactory treatment outcomes, which is where ketamine comes in, with the potential to provide immediate relief for up to weeks at a time.

Many people say that within a few sessions, they have found relief from depressive symptoms that last for weeks, even months. This positions ketamine as an effective alternative for individuals with treatment-resistant depression.

## **The Role of Psychotherapy in Ketamine Treatment**

Although ketamine can be highly successful in alleviating depression symptoms, it’s essential to be aware that it is not a cure-all. Integrating [ketamine therapy with psychotherapy](https://www.isha.health/locations/online-at-home-ketamine-therapy-in-california) can greatly enhance long-term results, leveraging neuroplasticity that results from ketamine sessions. Psychotherapy aids people in understanding the underlying causes of their depression, spotting unhealthy thought patterns, and forming coping skills to handle subsequent depressive broods.

Many people experience the benefits of ketamine at a greater level when they are also actively engaged in a psychotherapeutic process. Therapies like cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), acceptance and commitment therapy(ACT), and internal family system(IFS) are often helpful. This combination enables people to find relief from symptoms and learn the tools to develop long-term mental resilience.

## **FAQs**

**How long does ketamine therapy for depression work?**

While one session of ketamine therapy may offer relief from depression for days to weeks, the length of effect differs from person to person. Maintaining the benefits might require regular treatments.

**Does ketamine work for all types of depression?**

Ketamine is particularly effective for treatment-resistant depression (TRD) in which other antidepressants did not provide relief. It may also help with depression that occurs alongside other conditions such as anxiety or PTSD. However, it doesn’t work for everyone, and its effects vary from person to person.

**Can ketamine be used alongside traditional antidepressants?**

Yes, ketamine may be used concurrently with other antidepressants, particularly for those who have not responded well to conventional therapies. However, it is essential to consult your doctor to determine the safest and most effective treatment plan.

**How often should I receive ketamine treatments for depression?**

The frequency of ketamine treatments varies. The standard approach involves 2-3 treatments per week for 2-3 weeks, followed by a personalized maintenance plan based on individual needs.
