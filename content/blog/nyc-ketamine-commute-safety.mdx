What is the difference between IV clinics and at-home ketamine in NYC?

IV Ketamine clinics (like Nushama or Ember Health) require patients to travel to a facility, undergo treatment, and then commute home while in a sensitive neuroplastic state, often requiring a chaperone. At-home ketamine therapy (like Isha Health) allows patients to self-administer clinical-grade medication in their own bed, eliminating external stressors and the need for travel.

## The Science of "Set and Setting"

‍

In psychedelic medicine, "Set" (your mindset) and "Setting" (your environment) are clinical variables, not just "vibes."

-   **At a Clinic:** The setting is sterile and controlled _during_ the session. But the moment you leave, the "Setting" becomes the MTA.
-   **At Home:** Your "commute" is the walk from your living room to your bed. You control the lighting, the temperature, and the noise level for the hours _after_ the session, which are just as critical as the session itself.

## Is it safe to take the subway after Ketamine?

‍

**No, it is not recommended.** Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic. Even after the acute effects wear off (usually 40-60 minutes post-infusion), your reaction times, balance, and sensory processing are altered.

-   **Sensory Overload:** The screech of a subway train or bright station lights can trigger anxiety in a post-ketamine state.
-   **Physical Safety:** Navigating stairs and gaps in the platform is risky while you are still experiencing mild ataxia (loss of coordination).
-   **Psychological Vulnerability:** You are in an "open" emotional state. An aggressive encounter on the street can be more traumatizing than usual.
