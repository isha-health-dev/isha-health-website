## **What Is Guided Imagery?**

‍

This technique allows individuals to develop peaceful mental images through their imagination. People utilize this technique to manage stress as well as treat pain and [relieve anxiety](/ketamine-therapy-for-anxiety). The visualization process happens under the guidance of knowledgeable experts who include therapists and meditation instructors.

‍

A person learns guided imagery by following [professional guidance](/locations/online-at-home-ketamine-therapy-in-arizona) to focus on their breath while keeping their eyes shut during a session. The guide uses a guided process where participants perceive a serene and soothing image. To manage stress, the practice requires people to see calm pictures or think about good things happening in challenging moments. ‍

‍

‍Ketamine therapy benefits can be strengthened when patients use guided imagery as an additional treatment method. ## **How To Do Guided Imagery For Ketamine Sessions**

‍

Individuals can do guided imagery before starting their ketamine session or practice it during its duration. Every individual should practice guided imagery as an anxiety reduction method ahead of their ketamine session. Most people use relaxing imagery or positive future imagery to enhance their ketamine session results.

‍

Each person can benefit from guided imagery when experiencing difficulty due to ketamine's dissociative effects throughout their session. The individual imagines peaceful images during the ketamine treatment session while also focusing on achieving a favorable outcome for the session. These are the steps to perform guided imagery while undergoing a ketamine session:

‍

1.  Choose a calming mental image. This may involve imagining a relaxing scene, such as a beach or a forest, or visualizing a positive outcome to the ketamine session.
2.  Focus on your breath. Take slow, deep breaths and focus on the sensation of the breath moving in and out of your body.
3.  Begin visualizing your calming mental image. Imagine yourself in the peaceful scene or experiencing the positive outcome.
4.  Use your senses. Engage your senses by imagining the sights, sounds, smells, and textures of your calming mental image. For example, if you are imagining a beach, imagine the sound of the waves, the feel of the sand between your toes, and the smell of the saltwater.
5.  Stay with the image. Continue to focus on your calming mental image throughout the ketamine session. If you begin to feel anxious or distressed, return to the mental image to help promote relaxation.

## **Scientific Evidence Supporting Guided Imagery**

‍

Guided imagery shows promise in reducing anxiety because of its proven stress management effects, but scientists have conducted minimal research about its use with ketamine sessions.

‍

The analysis of randomized controlled trials conducted by Richardson et al. (2007) demonstrated that guided imagery boasted effective results to decrease anxiety levels among patients diagnosed with cancer, along with heart disease or surgery (Richardson et al., 2007). Research by Gross et al. (2015) proved that properly administered guided imagery could decrease both anxiety and stress during dental treatment (Gross et al., 2015).

‍

Guided imagery shows promising results when used with other treatment methods for mental health disorders, according to available evidence. Guided imagery achieved successful results in decreasing symptoms of both [depression](/ketamine-for-depression) and anxiety in patients who participated in trials along with cognitive-behavioral therapy, as reported in Cavanagh et al (2014).

‍

Currently, there is scarce research regarding guided imagery approaches for ketamine sessions, but the available evidence suggests this method helps patients feel calmer and less anxious during their medical procedures.

## **FAQs**

‍

### **What happens during a typical ketamine treatment session?**

‍

The medication delivery for typical ketamine treatments happens through intravenous injection, intramuscular injection, intranasal administration or oral/sublingual tablet. Medical professionals monitor your treatment while you navigate the treatment course. When patients undergo ketamine therapy, they commonly experience two distinct conditions: dreamlike states with or without images, and gaining insights into issues that they have experienced or are experiencing. The medication sessions ranges between 40 minutes to 2 hours based on treatment requirements and dosage amounts.

‍

### **What can you expect after a ketamine treatment session?**

‍

Patients usually experience feeling calm and sometimes tired, which resembles waking up from deep rest after finishing the treatment. Many people are surprised to discover feelings or memories that they were not aware of and revelations that come through different thought processes and experiences during the few hours following treatment. Patients who receive ketamine treatment need rest time and are recommended to avoid driving, hard physical activities and social media usage. Patients usually show improved moods and decreased depression or anxiety symptoms, starting from hours to days, subject to their personal medical response.

‍

### **What are the steps to prepare for a ketamine infusion?**

### ‍

An effective beginning to ketamine infusion preparation requires patients to establish treatment expectations with their healthcare provider while sharing their therapeutic objectives. A  four-hour break in eating should occur together with a two-hour drinking restriction prior to your ketamine infusion session. Comfortable attire should be worn to sessions, while relaxation items such as blankets and playlists could help you feel more comfortable. Sporting relaxation skills or mindfulness training in advance will help you develop a positive emotional state before treatment.
