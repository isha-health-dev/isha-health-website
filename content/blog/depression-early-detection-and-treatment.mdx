## Why Mental Health Screening Matters

Mental health screening is an essential tool for identifying depression and other mental health disorders. It helps healthcare professionals detect symptoms early on so that they can provide timely treatment.

Screening can be done in various ways, including online questionnaires, in-person interviews, or self-assessment tools. These tools are designed to identify symptoms of depression such as sadness, hopelessness, irritability, fatigue, difficulty sleeping, and thoughts of suicide.

Early detection of depression can make a big difference in the outcome. It can help prevent complications such as substance abuse, social isolation, and suicide.

‍

## How Mental Health Screening Works

Mental health screening involves answering a few questions about your mood, behavior, and lifestyle. The questions are designed to identify symptoms of depression or other mental health disorders.

Screening can be done online or in person. Online screening tools are usually free and confidential. They are designed to be user-friendly so that anyone can use them without any difficulty.

In-person screening involves meeting with a healthcare professional who will ask you questions about your mood, behavior, and lifestyle. The professional will then evaluate your answers to determine if you have symptoms of depression or other mental health disorders.
