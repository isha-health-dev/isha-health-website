## **What Is Ketamine Nasal Spray?**

‍

The dissociative anesthetic substance Ketamine received its first medical approval in the 1960s. In 2019, [Spravato](https://www.janssenlabels.com/package-insert/product-monograph/prescribing-information/SPRAVATO-pi.pdf?utm) brand nasal spray delivered the S-enantiomer (esketamine) through a product from Janssen Labels called a precision metered-dose device in the United States markets. Esketamine's antidepressant function derives from its capability to modulate glutamate activity by blocking NMDA receptors, resulting in synaptogenic effects that define its quick action.

‍

Nasally administered esketamine works as a treatment option for adult patients having major depressive disorder after failing two distinct [antidepressant](/post/how-is-ketamine-different-from-traditional-antidepressants) therapies, while also treating adults with depression and acute suicidal behaviors. The pharmaceutical administration of esketamine takes place only in medical facilities under direct supervision because of its dissociative effects and psychoactive properties.

‍

## **Mechanism Of Action And Purpose**

The main effects of Esketamine function by blocking N-methyl-D-aspartate (NMDA) [receptors](/post/how-does-ketamine-work), which belong to glutamate receptors throughout the central nervous system. The blocking effect of NMDA receptors activates glutamate release in spikes and activates AMPA receptors to drive synaptogenesis, which leads to improved neural connectivity with mood-enhancing results.

The analgesic effects of ketamine alongside its adjacent stereoisomer derivatives apply to neuropathic pain and chronic conditions because they modulate central sensitization and lower nociceptive responses. The administration of Ketamine nasal spray for pain management requires dosages that are either low or comparable to standard patient requirements because healthcare professionals are creating official treatment recommendations.

## **Dosage And Administration**

‍

According to Janssen, treatment typically begins with twice-weekly doses for four weeks, followed by weekly or biweekly maintenance depending on response and tolerability. A single device contains 28 mg of medication, so the administration requires two devices for 56 mg and three for 84 mg.

‍

Patients self-administer the nasal spray under the supervision of [trained healthcare staff](/locations/online-at-home-ketamine-therapy-in-new-york) in a REMS-certified setting. An entire administration session includes 1.5 to 2 hours for medication delivery and recovery while providers monitor patient status during the period. Two hours of continuous monitoring follow the dosage to identify adverse effects, including sedation, [dissociation, or high blood pressure](https://www.mayoclinic.org/drugs-supplements/esketamine-nasal-route/description/drg-20458442?).

## **Onset, Peak, And Duration Of Acute Effects**

‍

Patients experience psychoactive effects from esketamine beginning 10 to 20 minutes after use, with maximum intensity reached in 20 to 40 minutes, lasting between 60 and 90 minutes.

‍

Esketamine develops its active effects through the nasal route 10 to 20 minutes after administration and reaches its peak effects at 20 to 40 minutes following dose time for approximately [60 to 90 minutes](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2023.1279657/full?utm). Per REMS guidelines, patients should not drive or operate heavy machinery until the day after treatment.

‍

The dissociative and sedative acute effects usually disappear within hours, but patients might encounter smaller sedation levels together with perception alterations during the first five hours after taking the drug. The pivotal trials conducted showed no cases of respiratory depression, yet healthcare providers keep patients under respiratory monitoring as a routine safety measure.

## **Duration Of Antidepressant Benefits**

The question arises, “How long does ketamine nasal spray last?” The simple answer is that, according to clinical research and medical practice reports, Esketamine nasal spray's antidepressant impact extends from 5 to 7 days. However, patient responses also depend on depression severity, metabolism, and [medication dosage](https://mcleanntc.com/how-long-does-ketamine-nasal-spray-last/?utm). Research on a specified patient group demonstrated positive therapeutic effects against placebo, which appeared within two to four hours of the intervention period and continued until day 28.

‍

Relapse prevention for depression patients occurs through extended maintenance therapy exceeding six months, which produces long-term effectiveness while keeping patients in treatment remission for two years or longer using regular dosing protocols as reported by PMC. Relapse can occur as early as 7 to 10 days after stopping treatment.

The blocking activity of ketamine on NMDA receptors helps decrease chronic pain disorders that stem from central sensitization and “wind-up” processes. Studies with substantial patient numbers need to be established to validate ketamine treatment effectiveness, while health providers specialized in pain management should lead off-label cases because of their knowledge regarding ketamine's dissociative properties and its impact on blood circulation.
