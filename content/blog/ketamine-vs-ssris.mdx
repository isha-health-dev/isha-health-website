## **Understanding SSRIs**

They are Медикаменты drugs used to treat depressive and [anxiety disorders](/ketamine-for-anxiety), Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors (SSRIs). They function by preventing serotonin reuptake in the brain, increasing the amount of it available in the synaptic cleft and thus aiding in mood and anxiety regulation.

-   **Common SSRIs:** Fluoxetine(Prozac), Sertraline (Zoloft), Escitalopram (Lexapro), Paroxetine (Paxil).
-   **Onset & Duration:** Full therapeutic effect usually takes 4–6 weeks.
-   **Side Effects:** Nausea, insomnia, sexual dysfunction, weight gain,  and, in some cases, emotional blunting.

SSRIs are first‑line treatments because of their safety profile and robust clinical data. However, SSRIs are ineffective in up to one‑third of patients, a phenomenon described as treatment‑resistant depression.

‍

## **What Is Ketamine?**

Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic that was first synthesized in the 1960s. In sub‑anesthetic doses, it has demonstrated rapid antidepressant and anti‑suicidal effects in previously treatment-resistant patients who have failed multiple SSRI trials.

-   **Mechanism of Action:** Ketamine inhibits NMDA glutamate [receptors](/post/how-does-ketamine-work), resulting in an accumulation of synaptic glutamate, which in turn activates AMPA receptors, increasing neuroplasticity.
-   **Onset & Duration:** Antidepressant effects may occur within hours and persist for 1–2 weeks following a single infusion.
-   **Administration Methods:** Intravenous infusion, intramuscular injection, nasal spray (such as Spravato), tablets for sublingual dissolution, or compounded intranasal solutions.

## **FDA Approval & Off‑Label Use**

FDA-approved uses and off-label uses.. They differ.. The Applications of Ketamine in Mental Health. Spravato (esketamine) is indicated for treatment-resistant depression, and intravenous or compounded formulations are “off-label.” The FDA does not approve these off-label indications, but they are frequently administered with excellent results and under clinical supervision. 

‍

### **FDA‑Approved**

Spravato (esketamine) nasal spray is FDA‑approved for the treatment of treatment‑resistant depression (TRD) and depressive symptoms in adults with major depressive disorder (MDD) with acute suicidal ideation or behavior.

‍

### **Off‑Label Ketamine**

Though ketamine is FDA‑approved as an anesthetic, its use for depression (other than with Spravato) is off‑label. Many clinicians give IV or IM [ketamine for depression](/ketamine-for-depression) and ketamine mental health indications, though these are not FDA‑approved indications.

‍

### **Compounded Ketamine**

Specialty pharmacies can compound ketamine into nasal sprays or lozenges. FDA has not approved compounded formulations, and their quality may differ.

## **Does Ketamine Work?**

An increasing amount of evidence points to ketamine’s rapid antidepressant properties, presenting it as an exciting therapy for severe depression. Studies show that ketamine can relieve symptoms within hours, whereas conventional antidepressants can take weeks. This rapid-acting property is genuinely helpful for people who are treatment-resistant to depression, in addition to acute suicidal ideation. 

-   **Fast Acting:** Unlike traditional SSRIs, which take weeks to work, ketamine can help you feel better within hours of being administered.
-   **Patient Types:** Studies reveal a 70% efficacy in treatment response with ketamine infusions in TRD patients, even after three failed SSRIs trials.
-   **Suicidality:** Ketamine has demonstrated rapid reduction in suicidal ideation, making it an essential addition to emergency [psychiatric treatment](/locations/online-at-home-ketamine-therapy-in-texas).

## **Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy**

Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy (KAP), on the other hand, pairs ketamine with talk therapy in structured sessions that can enhance neuroplasticity and create the conditions for positive changes. Ketamine unlocks emotional depths to examine in-depth with a [trained therapist](/locations/online-at-home-ketamine-therapy-in-colorado) during treatment. This integration facilitates durable psychological transformation, providing relief for conditions such as depression, anxiety, and PTSD.

-   **Assessment:** Medical and psychiatric screening will be performed to assess suitability.
-   **Dosing Session:** Patients have ketamine (infusion, injection, or nasal spray) administered in a therapeutic setting.
-   **Therapeutic Help:** A therapist assists the patient in navigating the dissociative state while unearthing and processing certain emotions.
-   **Integration:** OCT-follow‑up psychotherapy sessions to bring insights into relief and sustain new pathways.

Research also suggests that ketamine plus psychotherapy could make [antidepressant](/post/how-is-ketamine-different-from-traditional-antidepressants) effects last longer than treatment alone.

### **SSRIs For Anxiety**

SSRIs are FDA‑approved for generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), social anxiety, and panic disorder. They lower baseline anxiety over weeks, but can cause a spike in anxiety in some patients in the short term.

## **Safety, Risks, And Best Practices**

-   **SSRIs:** Safe in general, but long-term use can result in sexual dysfunction, weight gain, and emotional blunting. Discontinuation syndrome can occur from withdrawal.
-   **Ketamine:** Short‑term side effects can include dissociation, increased blood pressure, and nausea. There are potential long-term risks associated with the use of high doses over time, including urinary toxicity and dependence.

-   Extensive evaluation for medical and psychiatric contraindications.
-   Ketamine use in medically supervised settings or through reputable telehealth programs.
-   Integration therapy to help retention of benefits.

### **Is ketamine more effective than SSRIs for depression?**

Ketamine is uniquely effective for treatment-resistant depression, providing rapid alleviation of even the most severe debilitating symptoms. At the same time, SSRIs are better used for long-term management of mild to moderate depression.

‍

### **How fast does ketamine work compared to SSRIs?**

Ketamine can also offer relief within hours, while SSRIs take weeks to record significant public health benefits in metrics of depressive symptoms.

‍

### **Can ketamine replace traditional antidepressants like SSRIs?**

While ketamine can help supplement SSRIs for some individuals, it's in no way a straight replacement as it works on different aspects of depression. It is only considered for the treatment of more severe cases.
