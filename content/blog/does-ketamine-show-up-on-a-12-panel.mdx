## **Does Ketamine Show Up On A DOT Drug Test?**

тАН

Drug testing is under the U.S Department of Transportation (DOT) regulations (49 CFR Part 40) for five classes only. Marijuana, cocaine, opiates, amphetamines, and phencyclidine (PCP). These chemicals are most important for testing in safety-sensitive industries like transportation, aviation, and trucking.┬а┬а┬а┬а

тАН

Ketamine is not in this standard panel. Therefore, DOT-mandated drug tests will not detect this drug without a special, expanded laboratory assay requested. However, businesses that operate under DOT regulations are limited by the Federal Test Guidelines and can not add substances, such as Ketamine, to the testing methodology without being explicitly allowed to by the DOT. In such cases, a separate, non-DOT test must be accomplished for ketamine testing for compliance purposes.┬а
