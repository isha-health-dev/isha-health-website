The resurgence of psychedelic research is changing how we understand mental health treatment. ## What Is Neuroplasticity, and Why Does It Matter?

Neuroplasticity is the foundation of learning, memory, and emotional resilience. Traditional antidepressants, including SSRIs, have long been believed to work by slowly promoting neural adaptations over weeks or months. However, psychedelics seem to fast-track this process, inducing profound structural and functional changes in the brain within hours to days.

According to the study, psychedelics activate **5-HT2A receptors**, stimulating the release of **brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF)**‚Äîa key protein that supports neuron growth and connectivity. These mechanisms help reshape thought patterns, potentially offering relief for those struggling with depression, PTSD, and anxiety.

## Key Findings: How Psychedelics Rewire the Brain

This extensive review analyzed **70 studies** and uncovered several groundbreaking insights:

-   **Psilocybin & Neuroplasticity**: Research on animal models found that a **single dose** of psilocybin increases the density of dendritic spines‚Äîthe structures that facilitate neuron-to-neuron communication‚Äîlasting for at least a month.
-   **LSD & Brain Rewiring**: Long-term LSD use correlates with increased cortical connectivity, supporting its potential for treating mental health disorders.
-   **DMT & Regenerative Effects**: Studies show that **DMT and ayahuasca** not only enhance neuroplasticity but also promote neuron survival, making them promising candidates for neurodegenerative conditions.
-   **Ketamine & Psychedelics: A Common Pathway?**: Ketamine, a legally prescribed treatment for depression, also triggers BDNF release, hinting at a shared mechanism with classic psychedelics.
