We at Isha Health are thrilled to announce our partnership with ATMA Journey Centers, a leading institution in Ketamine-Assisted Psychotherapy (KAP) training across North America. This collaboration provides clinicians with the opportunity to receive high-quality KAP certification from the top organization in the field for $1,980.

Since its inception, Isha Health has been committed to making ketamine-assisted psychotherapy accessible to patients from diverse backgrounds. One of the biggest challenges has been providing psychotherapists who serve these populations with the necessary, top-tier training. This partnership with ATMA addresses this challenge by offering accessible, high-quality training to licensed psychotherapists, regardless of their background or the communities they serve.

ATMA Journey Centers is recognized as a leader in psychedelic-assisted psychotherapy training in North America, having trained hundreds of licensed clinicians. Their program not only offers rigorous, industry-leading courses but also provides access to a network of practitioners who provide ketamine assisted psychotherapy.

Through this partnership, licensed psychotherapists who enroll in ATMA's KAP Training program via Isha Health will gain access to this world-class training for $1,980—significantly lower than the $6,000-8,000 typically charged by other organizations, which has often restricted opportunities to a limited number of psychotherapists. This initiative underscores our shared commitment to making high-quality training accessible to therapists and patients from all walks of life.

## **About ATMA Journey Centers Inc.**

ATMA is a Canadian company dedicated to delivering effective, innovative healing and transformative experiences through the potential of psychedelic medicine. Their mission is to awaken the inner healer and foster a deeper connection with oneself, others, and the world. ATMA was the first private company in Canada to conduct legal psychedelic-assisted therapy with psilocybin, one of the active compounds found in 'magic mushrooms.'
