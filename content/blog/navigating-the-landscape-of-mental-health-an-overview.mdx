## **The Limitations of Current Mental Health Treatment**

### **1\. Affordability and Accessibility**

Traditional mental health treatments, including therapy and medication, can be expensive and often require insurance coverage. For many individuals, the financial burden becomes a significant barrier to seeking help. Moreover, accessibility to mental health professionals can be limited, especially in rural or remote areas, leaving numerous individuals without the support they require.

‍

### **2\. Long Waiting Periods**

The demand for mental health services often outweighs the supply of mental health professionals. As a result, long waiting periods for appointments are common. During this waiting time, individuals might experience a deterioration in their mental health, emphasizing the need for quicker and more accessible solutions.

‍

### **3\. Stigmatization and Discrimination**

Stigmatization and discrimination associated with mental health continue to persist, discouraging individuals from seeking help. The fear of judgment from society, peers, or employers often hinders individuals from openly discussing their mental health concerns and pursuing appropriate treatment.
